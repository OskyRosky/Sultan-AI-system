# 04 Regime Context Framing

## Purpose

Regime Context Framing defines the market context assumptions that must be considered before rule construction and strategy composition.

## Placement

Regime context comes before Rule Construction and Strategy Composition. This prevents rules from being designed as if market context were an afterthought.

## Meaning Of Regime

A regime is a prior context descriptor used to frame candidate design. It may describe conditions such as volatility environment, liquidity context, trend state, macro phase, or structural market condition.

## Explicit Non-Scope

This layer does not implement:

- Automatic regime switching.
- Automatic market timing.
- Dynamic allocation.
- Real-time classification.
- Backtested regime filters.

## Governance Requirement

Any future regime framing must document why the context is relevant, what artifact supports it, and how it may later be falsified.
